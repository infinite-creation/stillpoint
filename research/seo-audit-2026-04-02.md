## Executive Summary

trystillpoint.life has a well-crafted, brand-aligned single page — but it is **not currently indexed by Google at all**. A `site:trystillpoint.life` search returns zero results, meaning the site is effectively invisible to search engines right now. This is the single most important thing to fix before any other SEO work matters. Once indexed, the site's biggest strengths are its clear, specific copy and a product category with almost no direct competitors. The top three priorities are: (1) get the site indexed via Google Search Console, (2) optimize the page's title tag and meta description, and (3) add FAQPage and Product schema markup to capture rich results. Beyond that, a modest content strategy — even two or three blog posts — would unlock keyword surface area that a single-page site simply can't compete for.

## Prioritized Action Plan

### Quick Wins — Do This Week

**1. Check for a stray noindex tag in Framer (30 min) — CRITICAL**
In Framer, go to Site Settings → SEO → and check every page to make sure "noindex" is not checked. A single misplaced noindex is the most common reason a Framer site is completely absent from Google.

**2. Set up Google Search Console and submit your sitemap (1 hour) — CRITICAL**
- Go to [search.google.com/search-console](https://search.google.com/search-console)
- Add your property: `trystillpoint.life`
- Verify ownership via Framer (paste the meta tag into Framer's `<head>` in Site Settings)
- Submit your sitemap: `https://trystillpoint.life/sitemap.xml`
- Request indexing of your homepage via the URL Inspection tool
- Expected timeline: Google typically acknowledges a sitemap within 24–48 hours; full indexation can take 1–4 weeks for a new domain.

**3. Optimize your title tag and meta description in Framer (30 min) — High impact**
In Framer Site Settings → SEO:
- **Title tag:** `StillPoint — Breath Anchor for Meditation & Sleep` (48 chars ✓)
- **Meta description:** `StillPoint is a soft, external breath anchor worn on the nose. It makes the breath easier to feel — so attention returns naturally during meditation and sleep wind-down. No apps. No batteries.` (193 chars — trim to 155)
- **Trimmed meta:** `StillPoint is a soft, external breath anchor worn on the nose. Makes the breath easier to feel for calmer meditation and easier sleep. No apps. No batteries.` (160 chars)

**4. Add alt text to all images in Framer (1 hour) — High impact**
For each image, write descriptive alt text with keywords:
- Product shot: `"StillPoint breath anchor worn on nose — external meditation tool"`
- Hand photo: `"StillPoint nose-worn meditation device held in hand, iridescent material"`
- Michael wearing it: `"Founder Michael wearing StillPoint meditation breath anchor"`

**5. Add Open Graph tags in Framer (30 min) — Medium impact**
Ensures correct preview when shared on Instagram, iMessage, etc.:
- og:title, og:description, og:image (use `stillpoint-social.jpg` from your images folder)

### Strategic Investments — Plan for This Quarter

**6. Add FAQPage schema markup — High impact, Moderate effort**
The site's FAQ section is a goldmine for Google's "People Also Ask" results. Adding FAQPage JSON-LD schema can get StillPoint questions appearing in Google search results even before you have strong organic rankings.
- Add the schema in Framer via a custom code block in Site Settings → `<head>`
- Target questions: "Does it block breathing?", "Is it a nasal dilator?", "Does it go inside the nostrils?" — these are natural "People Also Ask" candidates

**7. Add Product schema markup — High impact, Moderate effort**
Structured data for Product tells Google exactly what you're selling:
- Name, description, price ($24), currency, availability (PreOrder), brand (StillPoint), image URL
- This can generate rich results (price + availability shown in SERP)

**8. Write your first 2 blog posts — High impact, Moderate effort**
Start with the two highest-opportunity informational topics:
- **"What is a breath anchor?"** — define the term, explain the practice, introduce StillPoint naturally at the end. Own this phrase in search.
- **"How to focus on the breath during meditation (and why it's harder than it sounds)"** — targets a high-volume how-to query, speaks directly to your customer's pain point.

Both posts should be 600–900 words, link back to the product page, and be added to a `/blog` or `/journal` section in Framer.

**9. Build a simple blog/journal section in Framer — Medium impact, Moderate effort**
A single-page site can only rank for one URL. Every blog post is a new indexable page, a new keyword opportunity, and a new entry point for customers. Even 3–5 posts would meaningfully expand StillPoint's search surface area.

**10. Google Business Profile (if applicable) — Low effort, Medium impact**
If you ever want local visibility or legitimacy signals, create a Google Business Profile for StillPoint. Even as a DTC brand, it adds a knowledge panel signal.
