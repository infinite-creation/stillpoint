## FAQ Items

---

### Does it block breathing?

No — breathing remains full and unrestricted. But here's something interesting about how Still Point actually works.

Still Point hovers in the space just below the nostrils. Rather than flowing straight up into the nose, incoming air is gently redirected — finding its way in from two directions: along the back-bottom of the nose where it meets the upper lip, and from the front near the tip. These two paths together wash across more of the nostril's surface than a straight upward flow would, engaging the sides and lower portions where breath sensation tends to be richest.

That fuller surface contact is the amplification — and it works in both directions. On the exhale, warm air rebounds off the sill and rises along the sides of the nose, a second wave of sensation on every out-breath.

Cooler on the way in. Warmer on the way out. The breath doesn't change in volume — it changes in vividness.

---

### What does it actually feel like?

On the in-breath, you'll notice a subtle coolness that feels broader and more present than usual — air arriving from more directions, washing across more of the nostril's surface. On the out-breath, warmth rises gently along the sides of the nose as air rebounds off the sill. It's bidirectional: both the inhale and the exhale become more vivid.

Most people notice it right away. The breath simply becomes easier to find, easier to stay with, and easier to return to when the mind wanders.

---

### Does it go inside the nostrils?

No, never. Still Point rests on the *outside* of the nose. Nothing enters the nostrils.

---

### Is it a nasal dilator?

No. Still Point isn't designed to open the airway or change how you breathe. It's a breath-awareness tool—its job is to make the breath easier to feel, not to alter it.

---

### How does it stay on?

It's self-retaining by contour fit. Still Point's S-curved arms rest against the outer nasal sidewalls, and that gentle contoured contact keeps it in place — no adhesive, no clamping, no straps. It should feel stable and comfortable, not pinched or forced.

---

### Will it fit my nose?

Still Point is designed to fit a wide range of nose shapes by resting on the outer sidewalls rather than clipping to the tip. If sizing options become available, we'll add guidance here.

---

### How do I put it on?

Place it gently on the nose so the arms rest on the outer sidewalls. It should settle in with light contact and feel stable without pressing. On and off in seconds.

---

### Can I talk while wearing it?

Yes. Still Point is designed to be unobtrusive—it leaves the mouth, lips, and lower face free.

---

### How long should I wear it?

As long as it feels comfortable. Many people use it for a full meditation session, a brief wind-down before bed, or a few quiet minutes between tasks. There's no required duration—follow what feels right.

---

### Can I wear it at bedtime?

Many people use Still Point as a breath anchor for falling asleep and returning to sleep if they wake with a busy mind. It's designed to support calm, embodied attention—which can be a helpful companion at night. Comfort varies by person; use what feels good for your body.

---

### What's it made of?

Still Point is made from a soft, flexible material designed for gentle, extended contact. It's easy to clean and designed to be quiet against the skin.

---

### How do I clean it?

Wash with mild soap and warm water, rinse well, and let it fully dry before storing or using again.

---

### Is this a medical device?

No. Still Point is not a medical device and is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease. It's a simple, sensory tool designed to support breath-based attention practice.

---

### What if it doesn't work for me?

We offer a 30-day satisfaction guarantee. If you're not completely satisfied with your StillPoint, you can return it within 30 days of receipt for a full refund of your purchase price.
