## Email 2 — The founder story
**Subject:** Why I almost didn't build this
**Send:** +2 days after Email 1

---

Hi {{contact.FIRSTNAME | default: "there"}},

I want to tell you something honestly.

I've been meditating for years. And for years, I struggled with the same thing most meditators struggle with: the breath is too subtle. You try to feel it — really feel it — and it's like trying to hold smoke. You return, drift, return, drift. And somewhere in that drift, a quiet frustration builds.

I tried everything. Counting. Labels. Body scans. Different techniques. They all helped, a little. But the fundamental problem remained: the breath just wasn't vivid enough to hold attention reliably.

So I started thinking differently. Not about technique — but about sensation.

What if the breath itself could be made easier to feel? Not louder or faster. Just more present. More *there.*

That's what led to Still Point. A small, soft piece that rests on the outside of the nose — on the sidewalls — and frames airflow in a way that amplifies breath sensation around the nostrils, especially the lower region. Not in the nostrils. Not blocking anything. Just gently increasing what's already there, so attention has something vivid to return to.

The first time I wore an early prototype during a sit, something shifted. The breath was *findable.* Reachable. I kept coming back to it — not because I was trying harder, but because it was easier.

That's what I wanted to share with you.

More soon.

Michael

## Email 3 — How to use it (before it arrives)
**Subject:** How to use Still Point (before it even arrives)
**Send:** +5 days after Email 2 (~day 7 from order)

---

Hi {{contact.FIRSTNAME | default: "there"}},

Your Still Point is still a few weeks out — but I thought this would be a good time to share how it actually works, so you're ready the moment it arrives.

**The basics**

Still Point is worn on the outside of the nose. It anchors on the outer sidewalls — the soft cartilage just to the sides of your nostrils — while floating gently around the tip. It stays in place through contour fit, not pressure. No pinching. No discomfort. Most people stop noticing it within the first minute or two.

It takes about three seconds to put on. On/off. That's it.

**What you'll likely notice**

Once it's on, you may notice breath sensation has become more vivid — particularly around the lower nostrils, where airflow enters and exits. This amplified sensation gives attention a clearer, more tangible signal to return to. Rather than chasing the breath, you can simply feel for it.

**A simple first practice**

1. Sit comfortably. Put Still Point on.
2. Close your eyes and find the breath at the nostrils.
3. Notice: is it easier to locate than usual?
4. When your mind drifts — and it will — simply return to that sensation.
5. Start with 5–10 minutes. No goals. Just noticing.

**It's also useful off the cushion**

Some people find it helpful at their desk, while winding down before sleep, or whenever they feel scattered and want a quiet reset. The breath is always there. Still Point just makes it easier to find.

I'll share more before it ships. Can't wait for you to try it.

## Email 4 — Behind the scenes
**Subject:** A look inside: Still Point in production
**Send:** +7 days after Email 3 (~day 14 from order)

---

Hi {{contact.FIRSTNAME | default: "there"}},

A quick update from the production side — and a peek at what goes into making something like this.

Still Point is made from a soft, medical-grade silicone. Getting the geometry right took a long time. The shape has to do several things at once: anchor firmly enough to stay put, rest gently enough to disappear into wear, float precisely around the nasal tip so airflow is never restricted, and frame the lower nostrils in a way that actually amplifies breath sensation.

Early prototypes were too stiff, or too loose, or anchored in the wrong place. Each iteration taught us something. The current design is the result of a lot of small refinements — most of them invisible, but all of them felt.

**A few things I'm proud of:**

The material is genuinely soft. People often expect something harder — a clip or a frame. Still Point is neither. It's closer to a gentle hug around the sides of the nose. Quiet, calm, and easy to forget you're wearing it.

The geometry is specific. It's not just a U-shaped piece of silicone. The angles and proportions matter. They're what make the breath sensation amplification work.

It requires nothing else. No app. No charging. No setup. You put it on and it does its job.

Your unit is coming. I appreciate your patience — and your faith in something new.

Michael
