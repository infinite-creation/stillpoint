---

## 1) Goal of the site (what “success” means)
Primary goal: convert qualified visitors into **purchasers** (Buy Now) with a clear, low-friction path to checkout.

Secondary goals:
- Convert interested-but-not-ready visitors into **email subscribers** (early access / updates).
- Explain what Still Point is in under 10 seconds.
- Build trust through clarity, simplicity, and visual evidence (on-face photos, close-ups).
- Answer the “Does it block breathing / does it go inside / is it a dilator?” questions quickly.
- Provide a clean path for press, partners, and practitioners to reach out.

---

## 2) Primary audience (start with one)
**Primary audience (v1.0):** people with busy minds who want a simple, non-tech tool to support meditation, calm, and sleep wind-down.

Secondary audiences (later pages / ad variants):
- Experienced meditators who want stronger breath salience for longer sits.
- Stress-sensitive nervous systems (non-medical “support” language).
- Practitioners (coaches/therapists/teachers) as a recommended tool.

---

## 3) Primary promise (one sentence)
Still Point makes the breath easier to feel, so attention can return naturally—supporting calmer, deeper practice without apps, tracking, or effort.

---

## 4) Core positioning (canonical)
Still Point is a soft, external, nose-worn **breath anchor** that amplifies the felt signal of breathing at the nostrils. It rests securely on the outer nasal sidewalls while floating around the nasal tip, leaving airflow unobstructed and helping attention return naturally through sensation.

## 6) Page structure (single-page v1)

### Section 1 — Hero (above-the-fold)
- Product name + one-sentence promise
- One clear CTA (waitlist)
- Primary product image: on-face photo, calm background, warm light

### Section 2 — What it is (10-second clarity)
- 2–3 bullets:
  - Worn externally; never enters the nostrils
  - Leaves airflow unobstructed
  - Amplifies breath sensation to help attention return

### Section 3 — How it works (simple mechanism)
- “Breath becomes more vivid → attention returns more easily → practice steadies”
- Diagram or 3-step illustration (later)
- Short “fit truth”: rests on sidewalls, floats around tip

### Section 4 — What it supports (use cases)
- Meditation (beginner to advanced)
- Nervous system settling between tasks
- Sleep support: fall asleep more easily; return to sleep after waking
- Travel / discreet public use
- Integration / emotionally intense seasons

### Section 5 — Who it’s for (and not for)
**For:**
- Busy minds, beginners, experienced meditators
- People who want non-tech support
- Anyone who benefits from tactile anchors

**Not positioned as:**
- Medical device
- Nasal dilator
- Treatment for anxiety/insomnia

### Section 6 — Proof / credibility
Pick 2–3:
- Founder story (short, human)
- Early testimonials (later)
- Practitioner quotes (later)
- Simple “why this exists” video (later)

### Section 7 — FAQ (must-answer questions)
- Does it block breathing?
- Does it go inside the nostrils?
- Is it a nasal dilator?
- Can I talk while wearing it?
- How do I clean it?
- How long do I wear it?
- What size is it / will it fit my nose?
- Is it comfortable for bedtime?
- What is your return policy? (when live)

### Section 8 — Final CTA
Repeat CTA + reassurance:
- “Join the waitlist for early access and launch pricing.”
- Optional: “We respect your inbox.”

---

## 7) Visual direction (v1)
- Close-ups: hand holding device, soft light, calm textures (linen/wood/skin/sunlight)
- On-face photos: neutral background, minimal distraction
- Color: warm neutrals; device color can be featured as accent
- Mood: calm, minimal, premium, friendly (not clinical)

---

## 8) Voice & words (align with canon)
- Quiet, clear, warm, minimal
- Use “supports / helps / designed to”
- Avoid medical claims and guarantees
- Avoid: “treats anxiety,” “cures insomnia,” “opens airway,” “clinically proven”

---

## 9) Primary CTA + funnel (v1)
**CTA:** Join the waitlist / Get early access  
**Capture:** Email only (name optional)  
**Thank-you page:** short founder message + what to expect + invite to reply with questions
