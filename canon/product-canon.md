## Canon statement (official)
Still Point is a soft, external, nose-worn breath-awareness amplifier. It anchors on the outer nasal sidewalls and intentionally floats around the nasal tip, leaving the nostrils fully unobstructed. This geometry frames airflow and increases sensation around the nostrils—especially the lower region—so the breath becomes more vivid and easier to follow.

## Non-negotiables (never break these)
- **External-only:** never enters the nostrils.
- **Unobstructed breathing:** preserves full airflow.
- **Primary anchor = sidewalls:** stability comes from the alar sidewalls, not the tip.
- **Tip float zone:** the tip is intentionally not clamped; space is part of the function.
- **Gentle retention:** resting, not pinching.
- **Instant usability:** on/off in seconds; no setup.
- **Practice-first:** supports attention; does not demand attention.

## Fit + contact model (standard language)
- **Primary anchor:** outer nostril wings / alar sidewalls (left/right), via gentle surface contact.
- **Float zone:** nasal tip and lower tip/sides remain free, allowing airflow to wash those areas.
- **Lower span:** hovers near the philtrum/upper lip line; minimal to no interference with speech or expression.
- **Breath channel:** open interior forms a clear channel in front of each nostril.

## Mechanism of action (canon, concise)
By anchoring on the sidewalls while floating around the tip, Still Point preserves airflow and increases sensory stimulation around the nostrils—particularly the lower portion—helping attention return naturally through amplified breath salience.

## What users should feel (experience targets)
- More vivid breath sensation at the nostrils.
- Faster reorientation when the mind drifts.
- A “quieting” effect through steadier attention.
- Comfort that remains stable over time.

## What it is not (avoid confusion)
- Not a nasal insert.
- Not a nasal dilator.
- Not a medical breathing aid.
- Not a tracker, wearable tech, or performance device.

## Use cases (canon list)
- Meditation (beginner to advanced)
- Breathwork (gentle practices)
- Nervous system settling between tasks
- **Sleep support:** to help you fall asleep more easily, and to help you return to sleep if you wake in the night with a busy mind
- Integration/support during emotionally intense seasons
- Travel / public spaces where subtle tools matter

## Form language (design + marketing consistency)
- Soft silicone-like material; calm translucency or muted tones.
- Continuous U-frame geometry; rounded edges; no sharp transitions.
- Minimal visual noise; sculptural simplicity; friendly and non-clinical.
