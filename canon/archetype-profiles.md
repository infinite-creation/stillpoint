## Archetype A: The Silent Guide (Lead)
**Essence:** A quiet companion that helps you return.  
**Role:** Gentle guidance without instruction.

### How it behaves
- Never demands. Never judges.
- Simply stays with you, inviting return through sensation.
- Feels like a soft, steady presence for attention.

### Voice
- Few words. Warm tone. Calm certainty.
- Experience over explanation.

### Promise line
“I don’t push you. I help you come back.”

### Best for
Meditators, seekers, sensitive nervous systems, people who dislike hype and “hacks.”

---

## Archetype B: The Anchor (Support)
**Essence:** Steadying. Grounding. Reliable.  
**Role:** A physical point of contact that helps re-orient attention and interrupt stress loops through simple sensation.

### How it behaves
- Holds steady when the mind doesn’t.
- Helps you step out of spirals by giving attention a sensory target to return to.
- Supports micro-moments: before a meeting, after conflict, in transition, at bedtime, or when waking in the night.

### Voice
- Clear, practical, reassuring.
- Emphasizes stability, repeatability, and nervous-system friendliness.

### Promise line
“When you drift or spin out, touch down.”

### Best for
Busy minds, stress-sensitive nervous systems, sleep/rumination, beginners who need something tangible.

---

## Archetype C: The Ritual Object (Accent)
**Essence:** A small talisman that begins the session.  
**Role:** A threshold marker—putting it on means “now we practice.”

### How it behaves
- The act of wearing becomes a cue: drop in.
- Deepens meaning through repetition.
- Signals: “I’m choosing presence.”

### Voice
- Poetic, minimal, intimate.
- Emphasizes devotion, tenderness, and simplicity.

### Promise line
“A small act that opens stillness.”

### Best for
People who love ritual, embodied spirituality, and objects with meaning.

---

## Recommended archetype stack (canonical)
1) The Silent Guide (lead)  
2) The Anchor (support)  
3) The Ritual Object (accent)
